About


Though I doubt my horses would agree, when it comes to horseback riding, the descent is far more challenging than the ascent. You must watch foot placement, speed, and terrain far more closely when going downhill to prevent injury. In the same way, when it comes to analysis, the most rigorous part is not what most would expect; it is not answering a question, but asking the right questions.

In development (and in life) we must question everything. Is there a more efficient approach? More scalable? More secure? The list goes on. Programming to me is not just writing code, but performing constant analysis. I am also a firm believer that being a programmer is language agnostic; the logical way of thinking is the defining factor. Nonetheless, I do have some favorite languages: Python, PHP, Bash, and PostgreSQL to name a few.

I started programming at the end of my undergraduate career. While continuing to learn on the job and through continuing education courses, I gradually transitioned into development. Currently, I am working towards my MS in CS at UC Davis.

When I'm not writing code, you can find me exploring a new trail with my dog or horse, practicing yoga, or learning Japanese.
